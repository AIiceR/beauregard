FIV

I don't know a lot about Beau's history- the adoption agency gave me almost no infomation about his past. I do know that at some stage while living on the streets he was trapped, neutered and released, as the notch in his ear will have been trimmed to denote that he has literally had the snip. I also know that he has tested positive for Feline Immunodeficiency Virus (FIV).

FIV is a chronic illness, which is common in street cats. It's blood- borne and often contracted during street fights, when cats may bite or scratch each other. Like its human equivalent HIV, FIV leaves the infected cat with a vulnerable immune system. This means that Beau can't really go outdoors, as he may get an infection which could leave him very ill.

FIV cats are very commonly prone to dental issues, including gum disease. Earlier this year Beau was diagnosed with chronic gingivitis, and the vet decided it was necessary for him to have all his teeth removed. He's much happier now that he has no teeth, as his appetite is much better now he's not in pain. I know this to be the case, as he gnaws urgently on my elbow at mealtimes.
