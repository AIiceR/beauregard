the journey

On the 26th January 2021 I caught a train to Hamburg- at this stage of lockdown there were no flights from Dubai to Berlin. I hadn't left Berlin for about five months at that stage, and was overjoyed to sit on the train and look out of the window at snowy countryside.

My basic state as a human being is anxious, and it was to noone's great surprise that my epic quest to collect Bobo- now renamed Beauregard, or Beau- was beset by disaster. Twenty minutes before his plane landed I took a nervous trip to the bathroom, and left my handy in the cubicle. On Sunday the lost property office was closed and I couldn't go too far from the gate so finding help in the weirdly empty Covid-era airport was impossible.

But that problem would need to wait. I stood nervously in front of the gates until I heard a distant chorus of sad yowling. Eight stressed out cats in carriers were carried through the double doors, and there, in one of them, was a scruffy boy with a squinty eye and a notch in his ear. I transferred him into his new carrier and we made our way to the train station- it was snowing, and Beau shivered, wide- eyed and silent, all the way from Hamburg to Wedding, where we would be locked together in a snowstorm while he recovered from the stress of his journey, with our only means of contacting the outside world being Facebook Messenger. I can laugh about it now, but at the time it was terrible.
